The Real Picture
Why France — and why it might be for you
Not a brochure. Real reasons Indian students choose France — and the honest cons too.
💸

Incredibly Low Tuition

Public universities charge around 3,000 to 4,500 euros per year for international students. That is less than one semester in the UK or Australia.

~3,500 EUR/yr
📜

Post-Study Visa (APS)

After your Masters, you get 1 extra year to find work in France or Europe. After PhD, it is 2 years. A real pathway to staying.

1–2 yrs APS
🏥

Free Healthcare

Register at Ameli.fr and get a Carte Vitale. Public healthcare is almost entirely free — no expensive insurance needed.

Near-free
💼

Work While Studying

Student visa allows 964 hours per year (about 19 hours per week). Minimum wage is around 11.65 euros per hour. You can earn 600 to 900 euros per month part-time.

600–900 EUR/mo
⚠️

The Honest Cons

French bureaucracy is painfully slow. Day-to-day life needs basic French. Paris is expensive. Culture shock is real for many Indians arriving here.

🌍

Gateway to Europe

Schengen zone means travel freely across 26 countries. London 2 hours by train, Barcelona 6 hours, Amsterdam 3 hours.

26 countries

Where to Live
Finding housing in France
The most stressful part of arriving. Here is everything we wish someone had told us.
🏢

CROUS Residences

Government student housing. Cheapest option. Furnished, bills included. Apply immediately after getting your admission letter — extremely competitive.

200 – 400 EUR / month
trouverunlogement.lescrous.fr
👥

Colocation (Shared Flat)

Most popular among students. Share a flat with 2 to 4 people. Great for socialising and splitting costs.

400 – 700 EUR / month Paris
300 – 500 EUR elsewhere
lacartedescolocs.fr
🏠

Private Studio

Full independence but much harder to get without a French guarantor. Use Visale to solve the guarantor problem. Available on Le Bon Coin and SeLoger.

700 – 1200 EUR Paris
500 – 800 EUR elsewhere
leboncoin.fr
🏨

Private Student Residences

Fully furnished, utilities included, less paperwork. Best for the first few months on arrival. More expensive but easiest option when you first land.

500 – 900 EUR / month
nexity-studea.com

🔑 Visale — Your Free French Guarantor

Most landlords require a French guarantor. As a foreign student you do not have one — so apply for Visale (a free government guarantee service) at visale.fr BEFORE you start apartment hunting. This is the single most important housing tip.

💰 CAF — Get 80 to 200 EUR Back Every Month

After you move in, apply for CAF housing aid at caf.fr. The French government gives students a monthly housing subsidy of 80 to 200 euros depending on your rent and city. Most Indian students do not know about this. Do not skip it.

⚠️ Scam Warning

Never pay a deposit before physically visiting the apartment. Never wire money internationally for a rental. If the price is suspiciously low or the owner says they are abroad — it is a scam. Very common targeting international students.
