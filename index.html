The Real Picture
Why France — and
why it might be for you
Not a brochure. Real reasons Indian students choose France — and the honest cons too.
💸

Incredibly Low Tuition

Public universities charge €3,000–4,500/year for international students. That's less than one semester in the UK or Australia.

~€3,500/yr
📜

Post-Study Visa (APS)

After your Masters, you get 1 extra year to find work in France or Europe. After PhD, it's 2 years. Real pathway to staying.

1–2 yrs APS
🏥

Free Healthcare

Register at Ameli.fr and get a Carte Vitale. Public healthcare is almost entirely free — no expensive insurance needed.

Near-free
💼

Work While Studying

Student visa allows 964 hours/year (~19h/week). Minimum wage is ~€11.65/hour. You can earn €600–900/month part-time.

€600–900/mo
⚠️

The Honest Cons

French bureaucracy is painfully slow. Day-to-day life needs basic French. Paris is expensive. Culture shock is real for many Indians.

🌍

Gateway to Europe

Schengen zone = travel freely across 26 countries. France is central — London 2h by train, Barcelona 6h, Amsterdam 3h.

26 countries

Where to Live
Finding housing in France
The most stressful part of arriving. Here's everything we wish someone had told us.
🏢

CROUS Residences

Government student housing. Cheapest option available. Furnished, bills included. Apply immediately after receiving your admission letter — extremely competitive.

€200 – €400 / month
→ trouverunlogement.lescrous.fr
👥

Colocation (Shared Flat)

Most popular among students. Share a flat with 2–4 people. Great for socialising and splitting costs. Most practical route for Indian students.

€400 – €700 / month Paris
€300 – €500 elsewhere
→ lacartedescolocs.fr
🏠

Private Studio

Full independence. Much harder to get without a French guarantor (use Visale to solve this). Available on Le Bon Coin, SeLoger, PAP.

€700 – €1,200 Paris
€500 – €800 elsewhere
→ leboncoin.fr
🏨

Private Student Residences

Fully furnished, utilities included, less paperwork. Best for the first few months on arrival. More expensive but the easiest option when you first land.

€500 – €900 / month
→ nexity-studea.com

🔑 Visale — Your Free French Guarantor

Most landlords require a French guarantor. As a foreign student, you don't have one — so apply for Visale (a free government guarantee service) at visale.fr BEFORE you start apartment hunting. This is the single most important housing tip. Without it, you'll be rejected by most landlords.

💰 CAF — Get €80–200 Back Every Month

After you move in, apply for CAF (aide au logement) at caf.fr. The French government gives students a monthly housing subsidy of €80–200 depending on your rent and city. Most Indian students don't know about this. It adds up to €1,000–2,400 per year — don't skip it.

⚠️ Scam Warning

Never pay a deposit before physically visiting the apartment. Never wire money internationally for a rental. If the price is suspiciously low or the owner is "abroad" — it's a scam. Very common targeting international students.
